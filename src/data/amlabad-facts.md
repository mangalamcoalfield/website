# Amlabad Coal Project — Public-Domain Facts

## 1. Location & setting

- Amlabad is an underground coal mine (colliery) in the eastern part of the Jharia Coalfield. (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance, MoEF&CC)
- The mine lies in District Dhanbad, Jharkhand. (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance, MoEF&CC)
- Administratively the mine is one of the units grouped under Cluster X of Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL). (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance, MoEF&CC)
- The notified mine-lease area of Amlabad UG is 386.95 hectares. (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance, MoEF&CC)
- The Jharia Coalfield sits within the Damodar Valley basin and is developed in the Gondwana (Barakar Formation) coal-bearing sequence. (source: Geological Survey of India / public geoscience record, Bhuvan NRSC geomorphology & lineament tiles)

## 2. Project status & clearances

- Amlabad UG is covered by the BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance granted by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEF&CC); half-yearly EC compliance reports for Cluster X are filed in the public domain. (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance and half-yearly compliance reports, MoEF&CC / PARIVESH)
- As recorded in the EC documentation, the Amlabad underground mine is in a closed / waterlogged state (the closed mine within Cluster X). (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance, MoEF&CC)
- The EC record notes that the Amlabad XIV Seam falls within a fire-affected zone of Cluster X. (source: BCCL Cluster-X Environmental Clearance, MoEF&CC)

## 3. Coal (general)

- Amlabad has historically produced coking coal in the washery-grade bands W-I (prime coking) and W-II, consistent with the Jharia Coalfield being India's principal prime coking-coal region. (source: BCCL published Grade Books / CIL coal-grade classification)
- Under the Coal India / Ministry of Coal grade system, prime and medium coking coals are classified by ash content into washery grades (W-I, W-II, etc.), and their pit-head prices are set through notified CIL/BCCL price notifications. (source: BCCL / Coal India public price notifications and grade classification)
- Coking coal from the Eastern Jharia area is beneficiated through BCCL's washery infrastructure before use in steel-making. (source: BCCL public reports / CAG audit of BCCL washeries)

## 4. Coal bed methane

- Coal bed methane (CBM) and coal mine methane (CMM) are methane naturally adsorbed within coal seams; it can be captured before or during mining and used as a fuel, and its capture also reduces mine-gas hazards and greenhouse emissions. (source: general CBM science / DGH CBM policy framework)
- India's CBM policy framework was established in 1997 under the Directorate General of Hydrocarbons (DGH), and CMM/CBM development in the coal sector is pursued by CMPDI/Coal India. (source: DGH CBM policy; USEPA/Global Methane Initiative India CMM country profiles)
- The Jharia Coalfield is recognised in public assessments as a prospective CBM/CMM region, and BCCL has publicly pursued CMM capture and utilisation projects in the coalfield (e.g. the mechanised pre-drainage project at its Moonidih mine). (source: USEPA India CMM Market Study 2019; Global Methane Initiative CMM Country Profile — India)

## 5. Safety & statutory framework

- Amlabad Colliery experienced two major historical firedamp explosions — on 28 February 1921 (11 killed) and on 5 February 1955 (52 killed) — during its early private-ownership era. (source: ENVIS EIACP node, IIT (ISM) Dhanbad — Environmental Problems of Mining)
- Indian underground coal mines operate under the Mines Act 1952 and the Coal Mines Regulations 2017 (CMR 2017), enforced by the Directorate General of Mines Safety (DGMS). (source: Mines Act 1952; Coal Mines Regulations 2017; DGMS)
- CMR 2017 sets the statutory framework for classifying gassy seams by degree and mandates ventilation, methane monitoring and, where required, degasification measures in gassy underground mines. (source: Coal Mines Regulations 2017, DGMS)
- DGMS issues technical circulars governing ventilation and safety practice in underground coal mines. (source: DGMS Technical Circular on Ventilation, 2025)

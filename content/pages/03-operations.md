# Operations & Capabilities

## Section intro
Through the Amlabad block in Eastern Jharia, Jharkhand, Mangalam runs two commercial lines — underground coal, and the coal bed methane held within the same seams. Both are run on the same foundation: qualified statutory officials, disciplined ventilation and ground control, and certified equipment.

---

## Business line 01 — Coal Mining

**Heading:** Reviving the Amlabad pits.

Amlabad is a historic underground colliery in Eastern Jharia, part of one of India's most established coal districts. Mangalam's coal line is dedicated to bringing its pits back into safe, compliant production through a phased programme of revival, development and dewatering across **Pit No. 1, 2, 3 and 4**.

Restoring an underground mine is careful, sequenced work: dewatering flooded workings, re-establishing ventilation and ground support, rehabilitating roadways, and standing up the statutory and engineering systems a working mine requires — before a single tonne is reconciled. We do it in the right order, to standard.

**Positioning:** powering India's industrial growth through responsible coal.

---

## Business line 02 — Coal Bed Methane (CBM)

**Heading:** Energy within the seam.

Coal seams hold methane within the coal itself. Left alone, that gas is a hazard to manage and, when vented, a greenhouse gas released to the atmosphere. Captured, it is a usable, cleaner-burning energy stream.

Mangalam's CBM line is a distinct commercial vertical: commercially capturing the methane held inside the Amlabad coal seams and putting it to use rather than venting it. It is the same block, worked twice — coal from the seam, and energy from within it.

*Note: we discuss methane as science in our knowledge hub and CBM as a business line in general terms. Site-specific gas readings are not published.*

## How we operate — capabilities

**Eyebrow:** How we operate

**01 · Mine Development**
Driving and supporting underground roadways and galleries that open up the coal seam for production — the groundwork that makes safe, orderly extraction possible.

**02 · Underground Operations**
Shift-by-shift production, reconciliation, and structured reporting across every working district, run by qualified officials.

**03 · Electrical & Mechanical**
Maintaining DGMS-approved equipment and carrying out the statutory testing that keeps machinery safe and certified underground.

**04 · Statutory Compliance**
Operating to the Coal Mines Regulations, 2017 and the Mines Act, 1952, with qualified officials in every mandated position and registers kept current.

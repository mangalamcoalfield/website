# Safety & Compliance

## Eyebrow
Safety & compliance

## Heading
The condition for permission to operate.

## Intro
Underground coal mining carries real risk. We treat statutory safety as the baseline, not an afterthought. In India, safety in coal mines is not optional or aspirational — it is law, administered by the Directorate General of Mines Safety (DGMS) under the Mines Act, 1952 and the Coal Mines Regulations, 2017. Mangalam operates to that framework, in full, every shift.

## How we keep people safe

**Qualified statutory officials in every position.**
Every position mandated by the regulations — manager, and the supervisory and engineering officials beneath — is filled by a person holding the statutory qualification and competency for the role. There are no gaps in the chain of responsibility.

**Statutory registers, maintained.**
The records the law requires — inspections, ventilation, support, equipment, and the rest — are kept current and available. Disciplined record-keeping is how a mine knows its own condition.

**Gas monitoring and ventilation discipline.**
Air is everything underground. We maintain ventilation to standard, monitor for mine gases, and run the degasification and air-management practices appropriate to the workings. Ventilation discipline is not a system you switch on — it is a habit the whole mine keeps.

**Ground control and roof support.**
Roof-support standards and ground-control practice are applied as workings advance, so that the ground stays stable where people work and pass.

**Periodic equipment testing.**
DGMS-approved equipment is tested and certified on schedule. Machinery that is not certified does not run.

**Medical examinations and competency tracking.**
The workforce — our own and contractor — is covered by the statutory medical examinations and by competency tracking, so that the people underground are fit and trained for the work.

## The standard

**Zero** compromise on the standards that keep people safe underground.

## The regulators and the rules

- **DGMS** — Directorate General of Mines Safety, the regulator for mine safety in India.
- **Mines Act, 1952** — the parent legislation.
- **Coal Mines Regulations, 2017** — the detailed rules governing coal mining, made under the Act.
